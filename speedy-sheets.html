Frequently Asked Questions

How does SpeedySheets track time from Google Calendar?

SpeedySheets connects to your Google Calendar and automatically imports events as billable time entries. Each calendar event becomes a line item on your timesheet, matched to the correct client or project based on rules you set. No manual entry required.

Does SpeedySheets work with Outlook?

Yes. SpeedySheets supports both Google Calendar and Outlook Calendar. Your calendar events are imported and converted into timesheet entries regardless of which platform you use.

Can SpeedySheets use my Chrome browsing history for time tracking?

Yes. SpeedySheets uses a Chrome extension to capture browsing sessions tied to specific clients or projects. If you spent 45 minutes in a client's CRM and 20 minutes writing in Google Docs, SpeedySheets logs both automatically with page titles for audit-ready detail.

Does SpeedySheets track desktop applications like Microsoft Word?

Yes. SpeedySheets includes a lightweight desktop tracker that captures active window titles, including Word documents, Excel files, and other desktop applications. This fills in time that browser-only trackers miss.

Is SpeedySheets built for freelancers or teams?

SpeedySheets is built specifically for solo freelancers and small consultants. The interface, pricing, and workflow are designed for individuals who bill clients by the hour, not for enterprise team management.

Does SpeedySheets generate invoices from my tracked time?

Yes. Once your timesheet is complete, SpeedySheets can generate a client-ready invoice with line items, hourly rates, and totals. You review it, click send, and get paid faster.

How is SpeedySheets different from Toggl or Harvest?

Toggl and Harvest require you to start and stop timers manually. SpeedySheets works passively — it pulls data from calendars, browsers, and desktop sessions you already use. No timers to forget, no entries to reconstruct at the end of the week.

Is there a free trial?
